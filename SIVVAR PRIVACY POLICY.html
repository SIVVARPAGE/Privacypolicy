The Information SIVVAR Collects

SIVVAR may obtain the following types of information from or concerning you or your mobile phone device, which may include information that can be used to identify you as specified below ("Personally Identifying Information"):

User Provided Information You provide certain Personally Identifiable Information, such as your mobile phone number, push notification name (if applicable), billing information (if applicable) and mobile device information to SIVVAR when choosing to participate in various SIVVAR Service, such as registering as a user, accessing an IVR service, speaking to Third party Contact Centres through the SIVVAR app etc. in order to allow you use the SIVVAR Service, SIVVAR will periodically access the database of third party organisations listed on SIVVAR and query their database to ascertain if you are one of their customers before giving you access to some services as the need arises.

Log File Information When you use the SIVVAR app, our servers may automatically record voice prompts or certain information you supplied while accessing services via the Dual Tone Multi Frequency(DTMF) key strokes of the SIVVAR app. These server logs may include information such as your response from an Interactive Voice Response (IVR), voice prompts, Internet Protocol ("IP") address, mobile phone type and model, language options, start time/stop time, browser type, referring / exit pages, platform type, number of clicks, domain names, landing pages, pages viewed and the order of those pages, the amount of time spent on particular pages, and the date and time of your request. When you use the SIVVAR Service, our servers log certain general information that our application sends whenever an IVR is accessed, or if you contact any third party Contact centre listed on SIVVAR, including time and date stamps and the organisations you contacted.

The Information SIVVAR Does Not Collect

SIVVAR does not collect names, emails, addresses or other contact information from its users' call log or address book other than missed call notification during initial registration verification process and messages―if missed call verification fails. We do not collect location data, but users may voluntarily share their location with third party organisations listed on SIVVAR if the need arises.

The contents of calls routed through the SIVVAR Service are not copied, kept or archived by SIVVAR in the normal course of business. Third party organisations whom you access might, however, record your call for their organisational purposes, and would inform you before doing that.

The SIVVAR Service is meant to be a Toll Free Voice Service replacement (in an environment where toll free calls are difficult to access and people spend their hard earned mobile airtime in speaking to IVR systems before being attended to by humans after spending endless time waiting), using data service, through a user's phone (either via GSM network or wifi). Users click the service they intend to access, and such requests will be transmitted via data service to our servers, and routed to the intended organisation (that must be listed on SIVVAR). If the desired organisation is not online, the user gets a voice notification with adequate information to advice otherwise.

Once a call has been connected, it does not reside on our servers anymore. The call details of any successfully connected calls are not kept or retained by SIVVAR at the end of the call. Such details may contain date and time stamp information associated with the connected calls, and the mobile phone numbers involved in the connection, as well as any other information which SIVVAR is legally compelled to collect.
